    Navigating humanity for the ultra high IQ


It’s 3am. We are in Winston Churchill's old bedroom on the Amalfi coast - it’s the size of a small church and frescos adorn its vaulted ceiling. To my right are a group of trapeze artists cavorting on the California king. To my left are the technical leadership of three of the world’s top AI labs. The man of the hour who has brought us all together is in the centre pouring champagne with one hand, dangling an impossibly long cigar in the other. At 6 foot 2 and wrapped in Savile Row black tie his easy poise fits with his role heading a 150 person team of the most celebrated and highest paid people in tech. In the past 6 months he’s batted away eight figure calls to lead teams for Elon Musk, Sam Altman and a directory’s worth of hedge funds.

However it wasn’t always this way. As a teen his frame was wrapped in excess weight, his hands flecked with the paint of Warhammer miniatures, and his presence betrayed a deep uneasiness of existing in the company of others. To the observer they are two separate people and popular culture proposes that there exists a type of trade off between the two. Technical aptitude and social ease, one must come at the expense of the other. This is not true, pareto improvement is possible. What is true is there is a dearth of writing on helping those who are more technically minded - the people who ask why before they ask how - learn social dynamics. My friend walked that path largely alone by devouring management books by the crate load and countless dead ends. This guide is aimed at helping others walk that path in company. → Read Here
